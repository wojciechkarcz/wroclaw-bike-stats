Project Overview

Wrocław Bike Stats is a hobby project aimed at creating an online tool for analyzing the usage of city bikes in Wrocław, Poland. It is a "data sandbox project" designed for experimenting, testing, and learning various technologies related to data analysis, data engineering, and data science.

Why Wrocław?

Wrocław is the only city in Poland that provides open public data on daily rides from its bike-sharing system. That is why the choice fell on Wrocław—it had easily accessible data in the form of ready-to-use CSV files.

Where Do the Data Come From?

The data come from the official service of the Wrocław City Council — Open Data Wrocław. All resources related to the Wrocław City Bike can be found here: https://opendata.cui.wroclaw.pl/dataset?tags=rowery.

Why City Bikes?

From my perspective, "bike data" are ideal for a sandbox project because they open up a wide range of possibilities. Simple records containing only information about a single ride simultaneously hold time series and geospatial data. Thanks to this, we can:

  • analyze global metrics of bike rides each day,
  • analyze traffic at individual stations,
  • analyze the most popular bike routes,
  • check the rental balance at each station at the end of the day, which affects so-called "bike rebalancing",
  • predict traffic at stations and on popular routes,
  • analyze traffic and rentals in real time,
  • identify bike numbers that may require servicing,
  • determine where bike stations are needed or unnecessary,
  • and much more.

Plans for the Future

The current main goal is to launch an MVP — a simple system presenting data on rides for a specific day and for any time range in 2025. In parallel, I am working on recording and processing data in real time, which should appear later. After that, I would like to focus on training an ML model to predict the number of rides each day and the final number of bikes at stations.
